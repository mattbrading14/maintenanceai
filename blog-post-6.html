Preventive Maintenance Checklist for Rental Properties:
What to Do and When

MaintenanceAI Team · March 2026 · 7 min read

Reactive maintenance — fixing things after they break — is always more expensive than preventive maintenance. An HVAC system that gets its filters changed quarterly lasts years longer than one that doesn't. A dryer vent cleaned annually is a dryer vent that doesn't start a fire. A water heater inspected every year is a water heater that doesn't flood a unit.

The challenge for most independent landlords isn't knowing what to do — it's remembering to do it and keeping track of what's been done. Here's a complete checklist organized by frequency, plus a system for making sure nothing gets missed.

Monthly Tasks

Every Month
Test smoke detectors in all units (confirm batteries)
Test CO2 detectors in all units
Check common area lighting — replace any burned bulbs
Inspect exterior for pest entry points, damage, or vandalism
Check gutters and downspouts after heavy rain
Review any tenant maintenance requests logged that month

Quarterly Tasks

Every 3 Months
Change HVAC filters in all units
Inspect HVAC units for unusual sounds or performance issues
Check water heater pressure relief valves
Test all GFI outlets in kitchens and bathrooms
Inspect plumbing under sinks for leaks or moisture
Check caulking around tubs, showers, and sinks
Inspect garage doors and openers (if applicable)
Check dryer vents for lint buildup

Bi-Annual Tasks

Every 6 Months
Service HVAC system (professional tune-up)
Clean refrigerator coils
Flush water heaters to remove sediment
Inspect roof for damage, missing shingles, or debris
Check window seals and weatherstripping
Inspect foundation for cracks or water intrusion signs
Test all door locks, deadbolts, and window locks
Inspect attic for pest activity or insulation issues

Annual Tasks

Every Year
Fire extinguisher inspection (certified technician)
Fire sprinkler system inspection (if applicable)
Kitchen hood fire suppression system (if applicable)
Full electrical inspection by licensed electrician
Pest control inspection and treatment
Drain cleaning — main lines and unit P-traps
Replace smoke detector batteries building-wide
Inspect and clean dryer vents professionally
Full unit walkthrough at lease renewal
Review and update vendor contact list

The best preventive maintenance program is the one you actually follow. A simple checklist you use consistently beats a sophisticated system you abandon after two months.

How to Make Sure Nothing Gets Missed

The biggest failure point in preventive maintenance isn't ignorance — it's forgetting. A task that's not scheduled is a task that gets skipped when things get busy.

The most reliable system is a calendar with recurring reminders. For each task above, create a recurring calendar event at the right frequency. When the reminder fires, the task gets done. Simple and effective.
