The Under-$100 Property Management Stack: Run a Professional Operation Without the Enterprise Price Tag

MaintenanceAI Team · March 2026 · 6 min read

One of the biggest myths in the landlord and property management world is that running things professionally requires expensive software. AppFolio starts at $200 a month. Buildium isn't far behind. Yardi has minimums that make small operators laugh — or cry.

The truth is you can run a tight, professional property management operation for well under $100 a month. You just need the right tools for each job — not one bloated platform trying to do everything.

Here's the stack that works for independent landlords and small property managers managing up to 200 units.

What You Actually Need to Manage Properties Well

Property management at the small-to-medium scale breaks down into five core functions:

  • Maintenance tracking — logging requests, dispatching vendors, documenting what was done
  • Tenant communication — responding to requests, sending notices, keeping records
  • Rent collection — collecting payments, tracking who's paid, handling late fees
  • Owner reporting — keeping property owners informed if you manage for others
  • Document storage — leases, receipts, work orders, inspection records

Most expensive platforms bundle all of this together and charge accordingly. But you can cover each function separately — often for free or close to it — and end up with a leaner, more flexible operation.

Breaking Each One Down

Maintenance: The Hardest Part to Get Right

Maintenance is where most small property managers lose the most time. Tenant texts pile up, vendor contact info lives in your phone, work orders exist only in your memory, and owner reporting is a monthly scramble.

MaintenanceAI handles this entire workflow. When a tenant texts or emails a maintenance issue, you screenshot it and upload it. The AI reads the message, categorizes the issue, matches the tenant to your directory, recommends the right vendor, and creates a structured work order ready for your approval. Vendor emails are pre-drafted. Everything is logged for owner reports. Preventive maintenance schedules run automatically. It starts at $29 a month.

Rent Collection: Keep It Simple

For landlords with under 10 units, Venmo and Zelle work fine — free, create a transaction record, and most tenants already have them. For something more formal, Avail and TurboTenant both have free tiers that handle online rent collection, automatic late fees, and payment history tracking. Neither requires you to pay until you want premium features.

Documents: Google Drive Is Enough

You don't need a proprietary document vault. A well-organized Google Drive with folders per property and per tenant handles leases, inspection reports, receipts, and work order records perfectly. Name your files consistently — something like UNIT-101_Lease_2025.pdf — and you'll never struggle to find anything.

Communication: A Dedicated Gmail Account

If you're using your personal email for tenant communication, stop. Set up a dedicated Gmail account for your property management business. It costs nothing, keeps your personal inbox clean, creates a searchable paper trail, and looks more professional.

Expenses: Google Sheets Is More Than Enough

A simple Google Sheet tracking income and expenses per property is sufficient for tax time if you're managing under 20 units. One tab per property, every maintenance expense logged with vendor name and date, exported to your accountant at year end. No subscription required.

The best property management stack isn't the most expensive one — it's the one you'll actually use consistently.

When to Upgrade

This stack works well up to about 50 units. Beyond that, you'll start to feel friction managing rent collection, leases, and accounting across separate tools. At that point a platform like Buildium may make sense — but MaintenanceAI's Professional plan handles up to 200 units on the maintenance side, so you can delay that decision longer than you think.

The Bottom Line

You can run a professional property management operation — with proper work orders, owner reports, vendor tracking, and preventive maintenance — for $29 to $99 a month. The expensive platforms are built for large companies with large teams. You don't need them to do a great job.
